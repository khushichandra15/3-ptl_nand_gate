A small 3-transistor NAND gate based on Pass Transistor Logic that is optimized for high-speed and low-power operation is proposed in this research. A 2-to-4 line decoder constructed at 180nm with a 1.0 V supply demonstrates the circuit's functional applicability, while the proposed circuit is designed and simulated at the 32nm technology node using HSPICE with a 0.9 V supply voltage. In comparison to traditional CMOS , Transmission Gate (TG)-based and Pass Transistor Logic (PTL)-based designs, simulation results verify that the suggested NAND gate achieves much lower static power dissipation of 0.000019 µW, lesser propagation delay of 9.35 ps, and a reduced transistor count, allowing for a highly compact structure. Monte Carlo analysis and HSpice verification are used to make sure the design is robust. Decoders, control logic, and energy-efficient computation blocks are examples of low-power digital systems that can benefit from the optimized gate's exceptional power-to-delay trade-off.
